## üìù **Converting Your WordPress Theme to Static HTML**

Since you have WordPress block theme files, you'll need to convert them to static HTML. Here are your options:

### Option A: Manual Conversion (Simplest)
Convert your WordPress templates to plain HTML:

1. **Extract the HTML structure** from your templates
2. **Keep your CSS** (`style.css`)
3. **Convert WordPress blocks** to regular HTML/CSS
4. **Create a simple blog structure** with:
   - `index.html` (blog listing)
   - `post.html` (single post template)
   - `style.css` (your styles)
   - `assets/` folder (fonts, images)

### Option B: Use a Static Site Generator

**Jekyll** (GitHub Pages compatible):
- Converts Markdown to HTML
- Free hosting on GitHub Pages
- Easy blog setup

**Hugo**:
- Very fast
- Great for blogs
- Deploy to Netlify/Vercel

**11ty (Eleventy)**:
- Simple and flexible
- Works with your existing HTML
